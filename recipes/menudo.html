Menudo

Description

Menudo is a traditional Mexican stew known for its rich, red chili broth. It is typically made with beef tripe and hominy, slow-simmered with various spices to create a deeply flavorful dish. Often considered a weekend or celebratory meal, Menudo is a comforting and communal food that holds heavy cultural significance.

Ingredients

  • 900 grams honeycomb tripe
  • 2 pig trotters
  • 3 cups cooked white hominy
  • 10 cups water
  • 4 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 head garlic
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 5 stalks cilantro
  • 2 whole bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 3 tsp mexican oregano
  • 4 chiles de arbol
  • 115 grams dried guajillo chiles
  • 4-5 large limes
  • 3 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp evaporated cane sugar (or piloncillo if available)

Steps

  1. Tripe Preperation
    • If using bleached (white) tripe, soak it in several changes of cold water for a few hours to remove the chlorine smell.
    • If using unbleached (grey/brown) tripe, clean it thoroughly under cold water and trim any excess fat.
  2. Initial Boil and Aromatics
    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add 900g honeycomb tripe and 2 pig trotters.
    • Boil for 5 minutes, then drain, discard the water, and rinse the tripe and trotters clean of any scum.
    • Wash the pot clean and return the newly cleaned tripe and trotters.
    • In the pot, add 10 cups water, 1 large onion, 1 head garlic, 5 cilantro stalks, 1 tbsp black peppercorns, 2 bay leaves, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 2 tsp Mexican oregano, 4 chiles de árbol, 3 tbsp kosher salt, and 2 tbsp vinegar.
    • Cover, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 3 hours.
    • Turn off the heat and cool to room temperature.
  3. Processing and Straining
    • Remove the tripe, brush off any spices, and slice into ½" x 2" strips.
    • Remove trotters, strip meat/tendon, and roughly chop the meat.
    • Strain the stock through a double mesh strainer into a bowl, pressing on the solids.
    • Wash out the pot and return the newly chopped tripe and trotters.
  4. Chili Base Preperation
    • Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) with the rack in the lower middle position.
    • Tear open 115g dried guajillo chiles; discard stems and seeds.
    • Flatten chiles on a foil-lined baking sheet and spritz with cooking spray.
    • Bake for 5 minutes (until sweet/peppery aroma develops; do not burn).
    • Remove from oven and cover with very hot tap water to rehydrate for 15–20 minutes.
  5. Final Assembly and Simmer
    • Drain the chiles and blend with about half of the reserved stock until smooth (add more stock if needed for blending).
    • Strain the chili mixture through a double mesh strainer into the pot with the tripe/trotters.
    • Pour the rest of the stock through the strainer into the pot, pressing any remaining solids.
    • Add 3 cups cooked white hominy into the pot (If canned: wash and soak in cold water to remove the "canned" taste before adding).
    • Add 2 tbsp vinegar, 2 tbsp evaporated cane sugar, 2 cloves garlic, 1 tsp mexican oregano, ½ tsp ground cumin, and salt lightly to taste.
    • Simmer uncovered for about an hour, until the tripe is colored and the soup is thick. Check salt and adjust as needed.
  6. Serving
    • For best flavor, let it rest overnight.
    • Serve with chopped onions, cilantro, and lots of lime.
